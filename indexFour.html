Methods

Bryman & Bell (2011) wrote research methods can be and are associated with different kinds of research design. In this different kinds of research design, there are different frameworks which suited both to a certain set of criteria and to the research investigation. Bryman & Bell also described that there were five (5) prominent research designs being outlined:

  1. Experimental and related design such as the quasi-experiment;
  2. Cross-sectional design, the most common form of which is social survey research;
  3. Longitudinal design and its various forms, such as the panel study and cohort study;
  4. Case study design; and
  5. Comparative design

For this, the design is on the present study which is a longitudinal study. This study will focus to explain the connection between the economic output and the technological upheaval for small businesses in Kuala Lumpur in relation to time series data, which limit the analysis within Kuala Lumpur itself. Furthermore, it will enable to abstract from the small businesses heterogeneity, by which the comparative design and cross-sectional design are not feasible. Moreover, this study will enable to give further explanation on the effect of the technological upheaval to the small businesses. Apparently, with the limitation of the resources, collection of data in relation to small businesses in Kuala Lumpur with regard to the time-series will further give understanding on the link between the economic output and the technological upheaval in the short-run and long-run. The longitudinal design will be suitable to map the change from the technology with the economic output and causal factors over time.

Cobb-Douglas Production Function

Cobb-Douglas function will be part of the process of the deduction to the research problem solving by reviewing the existing theory, where the hypothesis are tested afterwards based on the empirical findings. The relevant theories can be revised which state at the beginning of the research (Bryman & Bell, 2007). The Cobb-Douglas production function is a particular functional form of the production function widely used to represent the technological relationship between the amount of two or more inputs (particularly capital and labor) and the amount of output that can be produced. As mentioned earlier, the Cobb-Douglas production function contain the following:

Y = AKαLβ

where:

Y = total production (the real value of all goods produced in a year or 365.25 days)

L = labor input (the total number of person-hours worked in a year of 365.25 days)

K = capital input (a measure of all machinery, equipment and buildings, the value of capital input divided by the price of the capital)

A = total productivity

α and β are the output elasticities of capital and labor, respectively. These values are constants determined by available technology. Output elasticity measures the responsiveness of output to change in levels of either labor or capital used in production, ceteris peribus. For example, if α equal to 0.45, a 1% increase in capital usage would be approximately a 0.45& increase output. (Wikipedia)
