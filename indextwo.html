The technology by no mean, is to be understood in the context of Cobb-Douglas production function, total factor productivity is captured by the variable A of which;

Y = ALαKβ

The equation means Y is representing total output, K represents capital input, L represents labor input, and α and β are the two inputs in respective of shares for the output. An increase in K or L will lead to an increase in output. However, due to the law of diminishing returns, the increased use of inputs may fail to yield increased output in the long run. The quantity of inputs does not completely determine the amount of output produced. How effective the factors of production are used is also important. Total productivity is less tangible than capital and labor inputs, and it can account for a range of factors, from technology, to human capital to organizational innovation.

Total factor productivity can be used to measure competitiveness. The higher a business's total factor productivity, the more competitive it is likely to be (subject to constraint such as resources). It is generally viewed as one of the main vehicles for driving economic output and its growth. When a business is able to increase its total productivity, it can yield higher output with the same resources, and therefore drive economic growth. This also can be associated with the Production Possibility Frontier (PPF) which define an increase in technology that allows for greater output upon the same inputs can be described as an outward shift of the PPF (Boundless Economics - Inputs to Production - Labor, Natural Reasources and Technology, 2020).
